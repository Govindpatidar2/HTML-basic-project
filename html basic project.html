The New Tata Harrier’s youthful energy meets warrior spirit that commands attention with its bold lines and stylish front grille, embodying a dynamic and confident presence on the road. Its carefully crafted details and purposeful design elements reflect the spirit of a modern-day warrior

Tata Motors has announced that its Harrier SUV has now crossed the one lakh sales milestone in the Indian market. The mid-size SUV was launched in India in early 2019, which means that it managed to achieve the feat in under four and a half years since its arrival. The five-seat SUV currently sits under the Safari as Tata Motors' second-most premium offering in the market.The Harrier is based on the Land Rover D8-derived OMEGA Arc platform, and is currently offered with a sole FCA-sourced 2.0-litre four-cylinder turbo diesel engine putting out 170 PS of maximum power and 350 Nm of peak torque. This engine is shared with the Jeep Compass, MG Hector, as well as its three-row sibling, Safari. Transmission options include a 6-speed manual gearbox as well as an optional 6-speed torque converter automatic.
